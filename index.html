Value Protection Scorecard

Our Value Protection Scorecard details the progress made with protecting production operations. The scorecard includes EVP, MAGICS, iRisk, OS Obsolescense, LSA Remediation and IT/OT Reliability.

EVP

  • 91% compliance for the year
  • 96% for the quarter, aiming for 98% by the end of the year!
  • All departments are also above the 90% threshold for EVP
  • Department schedules now in place to ensure we're always up to date with patching!

    • iRisk

  • 100% of all issues closed for iRisk High rated applications
  • For Medium rated applications 70% are at the final step (step 4)
  • with the remaining at step 3, we're off track for the end of October deadline. A plan is being worked to remediate the remaining 30% before end of November.

    • Magics

  • For MAGICS we are re at 100% completion for all toolkits for the JAS quarter but only 47% compliance
  • We are aiming 80%+ compliance by the end of the next quarter

    • Obsolescense

  • For IT devices we have 44.48% remediated. A plan has been devised to complete the remainder by the end of 2020. For OT devices our plan is clear and we're looking to wipe OS Obsolescence by the end of the year

    • LSA Remediation

  • LSA is 100% complete with 4 remediation actions to carry out. This will be done by the end of 2020.

    • ITOT Reliability

  • For October we've been green for every day except one day where we had DC downtime for several hours due to an error with an upgrade.

    • Enhanced Vulnerability Protection

    EVP plays a key role in protecting our production systems in London Plant, which is why we have helped to drive focus and ensure patching is done on time. We've increased our scores in all departments across all sectors (since 1st July), whilst staying vigilant of critical vulnerabilities.

    ITOT SPOC Forum

    In October we introduced our ITOT SPOC forums - a way of communicating with department OT SPOCs/E&I/PC&IS Leaders around the site. We spent our first session introducing ideas and sharing the wants and needs of the department leaders and ourselves.

    We'll be hosting these sessions monthly to ensure we're keeping fully up to date with our SPOCs on site

    Our Plan

    We started these forums as a way to for us to communicate our colleagues on a more granular level, allowing everyone to leave the session with knowledge on who to approach and how.


    Our Points

    We introduced ourselves properly, giving everyone an insight on what we do. This gave everyone an insight on who to speak to about a certain topic, as well as breaking the barrier between engineering and ITOT.



    The Changes

    We took the opportunity to give an update on our plans for the future. By explaining our projects and things to look out for, the attendees left with an idea of what we're working on, along with how they are benifitted.

    The Wants and Needs

    We also got our points across regarding what we're asking for, giving a backstory on what we need rather than sending out an email to get a point across.
